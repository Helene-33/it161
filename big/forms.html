Web Forms

Web forms are a great tool to offer the possibility to users to interact and put input on your website. Because of the work and the time you invested in your website, and the importance it has for you and your client, you want users to have a good experience on your website. Thanks to web forms, the visitors can “get in contact with you and send information, such as an order, a catalog request, or even a query, which is passed on to your database” (Puri). Web forms can be different types such as for instance a comment box to fill up or a list with boxes to check, and they can be constructed in different programming languages such as “PHP, HTML, or Java” (Puri).

Receiving feedback from your visitors can be extremely helpful to improve your site. Therefore, even if your site doesn’t require collecting information from your visitors, you should consider using forms. However, if you do want or need to collect information, then you need to be careful about the questions you will ask. They need to make sense to the visitor who might otherwise feel frustrated. For instance, it doesn’t really make sense to ask for the visitors mailing address if all he wants is subscribing to an electronic newsletter.

To build a form you can use the form element. While this element “does not actually create form fields, [it is] used as a parent container to hold form fields such as input and textarea elements” (Wood). The form tag is one of the most used tags as “without form tags, the internet would be a read-only repository of boring documentation” (Kant). Once the visitor of a website is using a web form, the data collected is sent to a selected server thanks to the action attribute.
