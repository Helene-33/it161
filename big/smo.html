Social Media Optimization (SMO)

Social media networks can be used in many ways, and one of them is to increase and manage a business or organization online presence: this process is called Social Media Optimization or SMO. While some are more familiar with SEO or Search Engine Optimization, the constant growth of social media is making SMO a strategic tool to “maximize [your] reach online” (Jackson). SMO is more than simply creating a profile on a social media such as Facebook, YouTube, Twitter or Instagram, it is about “strategically creating, building and maximizing your social media plan to connect with your target audience” (Jackson). Because social media are free and used so often, not using them to gain visibility for your organization would be a loss. Not only do they allow you to increase your visibility, but they also give you the chance to connect easily with your target audience and even a larger public and, therefore, are making you and your organization stronger.

But how can we use SMO in our favor? Well many marketing strategies are studying SMO and how to successfully apply it; for instance, the use of pictures and hashtags is very important (Jackson), as pictures are attracting people’s attention more than a text, and hashtags are constantly used on Twitter and Instagram for example to search and find something. Studies have also shown that the time you are choosing to post something matters, same with how often and how much you are posting (Jackson). Moreover, once your post is online, the people seeing it can share it and increase your audience over and over; “this strategy, called viral marketing, attempts to achieve a broader reach by getting engaged users of social media platforms to share content rather than relying on users to find the content on their own” (Kenton). Therefore, not only you are gaining online visibility, but the users are doing the work for you.
